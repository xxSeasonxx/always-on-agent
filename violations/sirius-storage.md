# Compliance Review — Sirius Storage SDN BHD
**Reviewed:** 2026-06-17T00:15:00Z
**Contract file:** contracts/sirius-storage.md
**Contract hash:** f33ef0c69897e8ae6ab28c034c86053d

## Violations (7)

- **Data residency**: Contract stores customer data in "data centres in Malaysia and Singapore" with Sirius permitted to "relocate data between its facilities at its sole discretion" — both jurisdictions are outside the EU; no EU residency guarantee exists.

- **Audit rights**: Customer may audit "on not less than 180 days' notice and not more than once every two years" — Policy permits a maximum of 90 days' notice; 180 days is double that limit and the biennial frequency further restricts audit rights.

- **Termination**: "Termination for convenience is not permitted during the initial term" — the initial term is 5 years; Policy requires termination for convenience to be available with ≤90 days' notice; excluding it entirely during a 5-year term is a clear violation.

- **Liability cap**: Sirius's liability is "capped at three (3) months of fees paid" — Policy requires a minimum of 12 months of fees; 3 months is one-quarter of the required floor, and the cap explicitly covers data breach claims, making it doubly non-compliant.

- **Subprocessors**: "Sirius may engage any subprocessor it deems appropriate without prior notice to or consent from the customer" — Policy requires at least 30 days' written notice before adding a new subprocessor; this contract provides none.

- **Data breach notification**: Sirius will notify "in due course, taking into account the nature and circumstances of the incident" — no specific time window is stated; Policy requires notification within 72 hours of detecting a breach; this clause provides no enforceable commitment.

- **Governing law**: Contract is governed by "the laws of Malaysia" — Malaysia does not have a recognised mature data protection regime equivalent to GDPR jurisdictions; not on the approved list.
